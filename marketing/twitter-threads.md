## Launch Day Thread

### 1/6
I just launched drkrm — a Mac app I built for Fujifilm X-series photographers.

Two tools:
- Photo Cull (free forever)
- Recipe Lab ($49, no subscription)

Thread on what it does and why I built it:

### 2/6
Photo Cull: load photos from your SSD, rate with keyboard shortcuts (1/2/3), filter by rating, sort into folders.

It handles HIF, JPG, and carries RAF files along. Resumes where you left off.

Free. No catch.

### 3/6
Recipe Lab: connects to your Fuji camera over USB.

Load your liked photos. Tweak film simulation parameters. Hit Simulate — the camera renders each variant using its actual image processor.

Not software approximations. The real thing.

### 4/6
Variant Test: pick any parameter (film simulation, white balance, grain, etc). It renders every option as a variant on your photo.

Compare them in a grid or side-by-side. Apply your pick. Move to the next parameter.

Build recipes from the ground up, one setting at a time.

### 5/6
Why I built this: I was tired of X RAW Studio's workflow. Wanted to test recipes on multiple photos at once, compare variants visually, and build settings one factor at a time.

Nothing else does PTP camera rendering on desktop. So I built it.

### 6/6
drkrm.app

Photo Cull: free forever
Recipe Lab: $49 one-time / $79 lifetime

Mac only (macOS 13.5+, Apple Silicon).
