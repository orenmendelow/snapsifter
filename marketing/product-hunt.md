## Description

drkrm is a Mac app for Fujifilm X-series photographers.

**Photo Cull** (free forever): Rate photos with keyboard shortcuts (1/2/3), filter by rating, sort into folders. Handles HEIF, JPG, and carries RAF files along. Resume where you left off. Fast — 3,000 photos loads in under 100ms.

**Recipe Lab** ($49 one-time): Connects to your Fuji camera over USB. Test film simulation recipes by rendering real variants on the camera's actual image processor — not software approximations. Compare side-by-side with synced zoom. Build recipes one parameter at a time.

No subscription. No cloud. Your photos stay on your drive.

macOS 13.5+ / Apple Silicon

## First Comment (from maker account)

I've been shooting Fuji for years and always found recipe testing tedious. X RAW Studio works but the workflow is clunky — one photo at a time, no comparison, no way to isolate individual parameters.

So I built drkrm. The key difference: it connects to your camera over USB and uses the camera's actual image processor to render variants. Pick any parameter (film simulation, white balance, grain size, etc.), it renders every option, and you compare them in a grid or side-by-side with synced zoom. Apply your pick, move to the next parameter, and build your recipe from the ground up.

Photo Cull started as a personal tool for culling trip photos faster. It's free, no gates, no trial. Just keyboard shortcuts and speed.

Recipe Lab is $49 one-time (or $79 lifetime). 14-day trial with full features.

I'm a solo dev — built this over ~6 months. Happy to answer any questions about the camera connection, the rendering approach, or anything else.
